E) Date & Location Consistency

Ensure all roles display consistent date formatting (Month YYYY – Month YYYY or Year – Year) and include either a city/state or "Remote" designation. For older roles, approximate dates are acceptable.

G) Education & Additional Sections

Education is well-placed at the end of the resume. If you hold any retail certifications, awards, or professional affiliations, consider adding a "Select Achievements" section — limited to 3–6 of the most relevant items — positioned after your experience section.

H) Hobbies & Interests

No hobbies section was observed, which is appropriate for a retail management target. If added, keep it brief and focused on teamwork, community involvement, or leadership-oriented interests.

I) Grammar & Style Corrections
  • Use consistent tense: present tense for current roles, past tense for previous positions.
  • Begin every bullet with a strong action verb (Managed, Led, Oversaw, Delivered).
  • Remove first-person language ("I work with," "I am able to") — resumes are written in third-person implied.
  • Standardize punctuation and spacing throughout.
  • Replace vague phrases ("strong leadership skills") with specific demonstrated outcomes.

Section 3: Resume Writing Best Practices & Job Search Support

A) Resume Writing Best Practices

The Value of a Targeted Headline

A one-line headline immediately communicates your expertise and role target to both recruiters and ATS systems. It acts as a professional billboard — specific, concise, and aligned to the job you want.

Crafting a Strong Branding Summary

Your summary is your personal commercial. In 3–4 sentences it should convey who you are, what you bring, and the value you deliver — using language that mirrors the job posting.

Writing High-Impact Bullets

Every bullet should follow the formula: Action Verb + Context + Result. Quantify wherever possible — numbers instantly make bullets more credible and compelling.

ATS-Optimized Skills

Incorporate keywords directly from job postings into your skills section. This improves your visibility in Applicant Tracking Systems and signals clear alignment with what employers are seeking.

Education & Certifications Formatting

List in reverse chronological order. Include institution name, degree/credential, and completion date. Place at the end of the resume unless you are a recent graduate.

B) Personal Branding, ATS & Formatting Tips
✔Keep formatting clean — no tables, graphics, or text boxes that can confuse ATS systems
✔Use a single consistent font throughout; 10–12pt body text with clear section headers
✔Tailor your headline and summary to each job posting for maximum relevance
✔Save and submit your resume as a .docx or PDF (confirm the employer's preference)
✔Aim for 1–2 pages maximum; prioritize the most recent and relevant 10–15 years
✔Include a LinkedIn URL in your contact header — ensure your profile matches your resume
C) LinkedIn Branding Tips
✔Use a professional headshot — profiles with photos receive significantly more views
✔Write your LinkedIn headline to reflect your target role, not just your current title
✔Add a custom banner image that reinforces your professional brand
✔Request recommendations from former managers or colleagues in retail leadership
✔Engage with retail industry content to increase profile visibility and recruiter reach
